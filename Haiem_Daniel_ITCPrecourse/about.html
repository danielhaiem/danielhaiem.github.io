Why Coding?

Growing up I never had much exposure to computers, not for lack of my age, but rather my parents believed that spending time outdoors in nature would be more beneficial to my upbringing. As a result, I had little interest in pursuing a degree in computer science. In fact, I didn’t know what I wanted to do at University all the way to graduation!

After graduating and entering the workforce, I quickly learned that being “tech-savvy” had many benefits, mainly, AUTOMATION! I taught myself Excel from a progression as beginner learning simple functions such as vlookups/xlookups and pivot tables to an intermediate knowledge understanding nesting functions such as index + match, to an advanced level mastering power pivot. This passion for automating tasks spread to learning Power BI and SQL too!

Fast forward to 2019, one year before my 30th birthday, I told myself that I wanted to do two things: first, move to Israel, the startup nation, and second to work in hi-tech. I didn’t know exactly what role I wanted to do, but I knew that I wanted to create something.

After arriving to Israel, I began Ulpan, Hebrew language classes, and had a conversation with my new flat mate/friend that sparked my interest in learning coding. He explained that during the period of early man there were those that started to speak for the first time and created languages to do so. There were also early humans who resisted to learn to speak and that those who learned to speak advanced. Just like in the past, today humans have created computer programming languages for the first time and those who learn to “speak” these languages will advance. I agree 100%!

There is a famous Chinese proverb that says: “The best time to plant a tree was 20 years ago. The second best time is now.” While I can’t travel to my past, I can start learning to code today and fulfill my new goal of creating applications and programs to automate things!

Experience

After graduating from the University of California at Santa Barbara with my bachelor's degree in Economics I entered the workforce as an accountant. During this time, I had the opportunity to learn a vast amount of accounting knowledge from how to prepare financial statements to ensuring GAAP compliance. I improved my Excel skills from beginner to master and as a result transitioned from accountant to financial analyst. I initiated projects that saved time and increased profits by automating tasks and providing detailed analysis. I held group training for colleagues, passing down my knowledge by teaching them tools and techniques to improve productivity. I look forward to applying my experience and knowledge to programming in my next position.
